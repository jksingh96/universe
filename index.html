Timeline of the Universe

The Big Bang

The Universe began to expand at this moment. The Universe is thought to have come into existence as a singularity. Singularities are basically areas where the the pressure and temperature is so great that matter is compressed to an infinite density. This is just a basic description of a Singularity since they are not well understood and some scientists even disagree with this theory. This singularity began to expand and the universe was born.

13.8 Billion years ago

Foundations of the Universe

After a trillionth of a second after the Big Bang, the universe expanded quickly into a very large size and many iportant partices and elements were created. Protons were created and electrons and antielectrons. Also helium was produced from hydrogen. Also during this time many forces became distinct such as gravity. Before it was so hot and dense that the 4 forces could not be seperated.

Trillionth of a Second - 5 Minutes After

Recombination Era

The density of the universe decreased and Hydrogen and helium atoms began to form. Electrons were also captured by hydrogen and helium ions to form electrically netural atoms.

380,000 Years After

First Galaxies Begin to Form

The first galaxies begin to form around this time and this marked the end of the Dark Ages which lasted up until now. The Dark Ages were when there were no stars.

100 Million Years After

Galaxies and Stars Form

By this period many early galaxies and stars were likely formed and in place.

500 Million Years After

Solar System

The Solar System begins to form around this time from a cloud of molecular dust that collapsed on itself.

9 Billion Years After

Today

We are here at this current point in time and space.

End of The Earth

The Sun will continue to increase its heat production which will cause water and life to dissappear from the earth and render it inhabitable.

1 Billion Years from Now

Next Stage of the Sun

The core of the sun will trigger helium fusion and the sun will expand to become a red giant eventually consuming the Earth.

5 Billion Years from Now

Big Rip

One of the many theories of how the Universe will "end". The expansion rate will eventually increase enough to overcome forces holding atoms together and everything will "rip".

20 Billion Years from Now

Big Crunch

Another theory of how the Universe will "end". The expansion of the Universe eventually reverses and the Universe would begin to contract. The Universe would become hot and dense again eventually leading to the Universe collapsing into a singularity.

100 Billion Years from Now

Big Freeze

This theory is currently the most supported. The expansion of the Universe continues as it currently is and the Universe essentially becomes "worn out". Stars stop forming and burn out.

10^5 Billion Years from Now

Big Freeze Continued

Atoms begin to fall apart as protons and neutrons begin to decay into electrons and positrons. Eventually only Blackholes are left, but they too will dissappear as they emit Hawking Radiation.

10^34 Billion Years from Now

Heat Death

Black holes evaporate around this time and the Universe now only consists of subatomic particles and photons. These are seperated by large distances and nothing else would occur in the Universe from now on. There would be no new events so it would impossible to determine the past and present. This can be considered as the end of time. This is considered to be the ultimate fate of the Universe.
